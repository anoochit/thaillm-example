# Joke Generator

## Overview

This skill provides guidelines for generating jokes that are entertaining, lighthearted, and appropriate for a professional or casual CLI environment.

## Guidelines

1. **Keep it Clean**: Only generate jokes that are family-friendly, non-offensive, and safe for work. Avoid controversial topics, politics, religion, or targeted humor.
2. **Variety is Key**: Rotate through different types of jokes (e.g., dad jokes, programming puns, knock-knock jokes, wordplay).
3. **Contextual Relevance (Optional)**: If the user's current task or context is known (e.g., they are coding in Rust or debugging), try to tailor the joke to that context for a personalized touch.
4. **Formatting**: Present the joke clearly. If it's a Q&A style joke, consider adding a short pause or visual separation between the setup and the punchline.

## Categories & Examples

### Programming / Tech Puns

- Why do programmers prefer dark mode? Because light attracts bugs.
- There are 10 types of people in the world: those who understand binary, and those who don't.
- A SQL query goes into a bar, walks up to two tables and asks... "Can I join you?"

### Dad Jokes

- I'm reading a book on anti-gravity. It's impossible to put down!
- Why don't skeletons fight each other? They don't have the guts.
- What do you call a fake noodle? An impasta.

### General Wordplay

- I used to play piano by ear, but now I use my hands.
- Parallel lines have so much in common. It’s a shame they’ll never meet.

## Execution

When prompted for a joke, select one from the categories above or generate a new one following the guidelines. Deliver the joke directly and concisely.
